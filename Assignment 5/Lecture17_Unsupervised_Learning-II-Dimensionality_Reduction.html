The eigenvalues reflect the importance of different axes of the dataset. The importance of different axes is directly proportional to the eigenvalues.

Here, the second eigenvalue is significantly higher than the other.

In other words, the variation of the data captured by the second principal axis is significantly more than that by the first principal axis.

Means of X:  [-0.27525325 -0.59676981]

Step 2: Calculate the singular values and singular vectors using SVD.

$$A_{m\times n} = U_{m \times m} S_{m \times n} V^T_{n \times n}$$

The shape of the dataset A is $100 \times 2$. Thus,

Matrix Shape
U 100 x 100
S 100 x 2
V 2 x 2

S is a rectangular matrix. As the shape of S is $100 \times 2$, there will be only 2 non-zero eigenvalues on the main diagonal of S. All others will be zero. Hence, the S is vector of length 2.

In [35]:

U, S, Vh = svd(normalized_X)

print("Shape of U: ", U.shape)
print("Shape of S: ", S.shape)
print("Shape of Vh: ", Vh.shape)

Shape of U:  (100, 100)
Shape of S:  (2,)
Shape of Vh:  (2, 2)

The singular values in decreasing order are:  [52.31194721  3.32744529]

Step 3: Choose components according to the singular values.

The underlying idea is to pick the components with significantly higher singular values.

NOTE: Since the first eigenvalue is significantly higher than the second eigenvalue, only u1 should be picked. However, we are selecting both to visualize them in a 2D plot.

In [37]:
# Only u1 should be picked. But we choose u1 and u2 for visualization.
u1 = U[:, 0]
u2 = U[:, 1]

Step 4: Form principal components.

In [38]:
np.diag(S)
Out[38]:

Let's see how PCA transforms the dataset.

In [39]:
# Reshape S to a m by n matrix
reshaped_S = np.zeros(shape=(U.shape[0], S.shape[0]))
reshaped_S[:S.shape[0], :] = np.diag(S)

# Project
components = U @ reshaped_S
U1 = components[:, 0]
U2 = components[:, 1]

# Visualize the normalized data
plt.figure(figsize=(8, 6))
plt.rcParams.update({"font.size": 16})

plt.ylabel('$u_2$')
plt.ylim(-10, 10)
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

We notice that the dominant principal component is u1. The data has maximum variability on the u1 axis.
